Welcome to my page! I'm a rising sophomore at New York University. If I'm not studying for my computer science degree, I'm usually in a sunny room sketching and painting or writing. I only started coding 7 months ago, and I am most proficient in Python, and it's my favorite language so far because I don't worry about semi-colons or private variables or declaring the datatypes of different data structures. I'm an artist at heart, so computer science does not come easy to me, but I love the thrill of discovering new ways to display ideas to other people, which is what drew me to pursue a career in this field.
The lack of diversity in tech also drew me to pursue a career in computer science. I'm a strong advocate of having fields of work that accurately represent the diverse and global population, and when I found out how little minorities and women were represented in computer science, I felt a strong desire to become a computer scientist to not only bring my creativity and build behind it a more technological background, but also to create a more inclusive field of study for others after me.
I have gone through a lot in the short journey of my computer science career. While maybe it's too early to be thinking about this, most, if not all, of my coding these past 7 months has been to complete assignments for one to two people to view in my college courses. At Google, I would love to create something that has meaning and purpose, where I can see my code impact individuals on a more significant level. Instead of creating a program that sorts the numbers in an array, I would like to see my code do something mroe impactful.
